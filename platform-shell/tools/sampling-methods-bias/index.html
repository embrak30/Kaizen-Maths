TEACHER INSTRUCTIONS
Teacher Instructions

Purpose

This tool generates practice on sampling methods, sampling bias, questionnaire bias, representative samples, and sampling calculations.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Name sampling methods, identify bias, and suggest simple improvements.
  • Level 2: Calculate stratified samples, systematic sampling intervals, and describe reliable sampling processes.
  • Level 3: Evaluate sampling plans, redesign weak methods, and justify choices in context.

Teaching Notes

  • A representative sample should reflect the population being studied.
  • Bias occurs when the method makes some outcomes or groups more likely than others.
  • Random selection reduces selection bias, but the sampling frame still matters.
  • Stratified sampling is useful when important groups should be represented in correct proportions.

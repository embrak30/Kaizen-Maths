Teacher Instructions

Purpose

This tool generates mechanics practice on work done, kinetic energy, gravitational potential energy, power, efficiency, and the work-energy principle.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Direct use of work done, kinetic energy, gravitational potential energy, and power formulae.
  • Level 2: Work-energy principle, braking force, power as force multiplied by velocity, and efficiency.
  • Level 3: Energy modelling on slopes, vertical motion, motors, resistance, and multi-step work-energy problems.

Teaching Notes

  • Work done is the energy transferred by a force moving through a distance in its direction.
  • Use the work-energy principle when a force changes the kinetic energy of a body.
  • Power is the rate of doing work or transferring energy.
  • For constant speed, the driving force balances the resisting forces, so power = force multiplied by speed.
  • On slopes, the component of weight down the plane is mg sin θ.

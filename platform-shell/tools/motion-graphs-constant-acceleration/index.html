Teacher Instructions

Purpose

This tool supports motion graphs and constant acceleration. It builds from average speed and acceleration into interpreting distance-time and velocity-time graphs.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Use speed, distance, time, acceleration, and simple graph facts.
  • Level 2: Interpret distance-time and velocity-time graphs using gradients and areas.
  • Level 3: Use constant acceleration formulae and mixed graph applications with missing values.

Teaching Notes

  • On a distance-time graph, gradient gives speed.
  • On a velocity-time graph, gradient gives acceleration and area gives distance travelled.
  • Deceleration can be shown as a negative acceleration or as a positive rate of decrease in speed.
speed = distance ÷ time a = (v - u) ÷ t distance = area under v-t graph v = u + at s = ut + ½at²

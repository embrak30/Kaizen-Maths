📚 Teacher Instructions

Getting Started

This tool helps students practise algebraic substitution in structured progression. Each level focuses on specific types of substitution problems.

Level Guide

  • Level 1 - Single Variable: Basic substitution with one variable
  • Level 2 - Multiple Variables: Substitution with two or more variables
  • Level 3 - Expression Substitution: Substituting expressions for variables
  • Level 4 - Complex Substitution: Multi-step substitution and fractions

Problem Format Guide

  • Given values: Variable assignments, for example \(x=3,\,y=-2\)
  • Find expression: The algebraic expression to evaluate
  • Rational numbers: Include integers, fractions, and decimals
  • Step-by-step: Shows substitution and simplification process

Level-Specific Skills

  • Level 1: Direct substitution with integers and simple expressions
  • Level 2: Substitution with multiple variables and mixed operations
  • Level 3: Substituting algebraic expressions for variables
  • Level 4: Complex substitution with fractions and multi-step processes

Teaching Strategies

  • Emphasise careful substitution of values for variables
  • Practise order of operations after substitution
  • Show how to handle negative values and parentheses
  • Build from simple to complex substitution patterns

Assessment Focus

  • Accuracy: Correct substitution of given values
  • Order of Operations: Proper sequence of calculations
  • Sign Handling: Correct treatment of negative numbers
  • Simplification: Reducing expressions to final form

Standards & Alignment

  • UK National Curriculum (KS3/KS4):
    • Substitute numerical values into formulae and expressions, including scientific formulae.
    • Use and interpret algebraic notation; simplify and manipulate expressions to maintain equivalence.
    • Apply and interpret the order of operations for calculations with algebraic terms.
  • Common Core State Standards (CCSS):
    • 6.EE.A.2c: Evaluate expressions at specific values of their variables.
    • 7.EE.A.1: Apply properties of operations to add, subtract, factor, and expand linear expressions with rational coefficients.
    • 8.EE, 8.F: Use substitution within simple function rules and linear models.
    • A-SSE.A, A-CED.A: Interpret and manipulate expressions and create equations using given quantities.

Using the Timer

  • 2 minutes for quick substitution practice
  • 5 minutes for complete solution with steps
  • Focus on building speed and accuracy

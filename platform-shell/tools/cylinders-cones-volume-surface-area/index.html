Cylinders & Cones Guidance

Overview

This tool covers volume and surface area of cylinders and cones, keeping full circles and sector work separate from this 3D topic.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Volume of cylinders and cones.
  • Level 2: Curved and total surface area of cylinders and cones.
  • Level 3: Reverse and mixed questions using volume or surface area.

Key Formulae

  • Cylinder volume: V = pi r^2 h.
  • Cone volume: V = 1/3 pi r^2 h.
  • Cylinder surface area: curved area = 2 pi r h, total area = 2 pi r^2 + 2 pi r h.
  • Cone surface area: curved area = pi r l, total area = pi r^2 + pi r l.

Common Misconceptions

  • Using the cone slant height instead of vertical height for volume.
  • Forgetting the 1/3 in cone volume.
  • Using height instead of slant height for cone surface area.
  • Giving volume answers in square units or surface area answers in cubic units.

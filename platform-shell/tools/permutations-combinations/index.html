TEACHER INSTRUCTIONS
Teacher Instructions

Purpose

This tool generates counting-principle, permutation, and combination practice. It is designed to help students decide whether order matters before choosing the method.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Factorial notation, the product rule, basic arrangements, and simple selections.
  • Level 2: Permutations with restrictions, repeated letters, circular arrangements, and mixed ordering problems.
  • Level 3: Combinations, committees, probability with combinations, binomial coefficients, and mixed exam-style questions.

Teaching Notes

  • Ask students to decide first: is this an arrangement or a selection?
  • If order matters, use a product rule or permutation method.
  • If order does not matter, divide by the repeated arrangements or use combinations.
  • Worked solutions show the formula and the substitution before evaluating the answer.

Common Mistakes

  • Using nCr when the positions or order are important.
  • Using nPr when a group is being selected and order is irrelevant.
  • Forgetting to divide repeated-letter arrangements by repeated factorials.
  • For probability questions, counting favourable outcomes but forgetting the total number of equally likely outcomes.

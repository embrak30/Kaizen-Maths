Venn Diagram Guidance

Overview

This tool supports CSEC-style set notation, reading Venn diagrams, two-set applications, probability from diagrams, and three-set problems.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Read and complete basic two-set diagrams.
  • Level 2: Complete two-set diagrams, probability questions, formula questions, and probability with algebra.
  • Level 3: Complete and interpret three-set Venn diagrams.

Teaching Focus

  • Always start in the intersection, then work outwards.
  • For two sets, use n(A union B) = n(A) + n(B) - n(A intersection B).
  • For three sets, put the centre first, then pair-only regions, then only-one regions, then outside.
  • For probability, denominator is usually the total number in the universal set.

Common Mistakes

  • Putting the total for A into the A-only region.
  • Forgetting to subtract the intersection from each set.
  • Counting the centre of a three-set diagram more than once.
  • Confusing "or" with "only".

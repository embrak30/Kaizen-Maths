TEACHER NOTES
Transposition of Formulae

Purpose

This tool helps teachers model how to change the subject of a formula through clear inverse operations.

Teaching Focus

  • Identify the variable that must become the subject.
  • Undo operations in a logical order, doing the same operation to both sides.
  • Collect terms containing the subject before factorising.
  • Use squaring and square rooting carefully, including the plus or minus case where needed.

Common Misconceptions

  • Dividing only one term instead of the whole side of an equation.
  • Moving a term across the equals sign without showing the inverse operation.
  • Forgetting to square both sides when removing a square root.
  • Dropping the plus or minus when taking a square root of a squared variable.

Transposition of Formulae

Generate practice for changing the subject of a formula, from simple inverse operations to brackets, fractions, repeated subjects, powers, and square roots.

log Teacher Instructions

Getting Started

This tool helps students master logarithms systematically, from converting between logarithmic and index forms to solving exponential and logarithmic equations.

Level Guide

  • Level 1 - Fundamentals: Log-index conversion and basic exact evaluation
  • Level 2 - Laws: Product, quotient, power, and natural logarithm rules
  • Level 3 - Equations: Exponential equations, logarithmic equations, change of base, and multi-step examples

Key Logarithm Laws

  • Definition: loga(x) = n means an = x
  • Product Rule: loga(xy) = loga(x) + loga(y)
  • Quotient Rule: loga(x/y) = loga(x) - loga(y)
  • Power Rule: loga(xn) = n loga(x)
  • Change of Base: loga(x) = logb(x) / logb(a)

Teaching Strategies

  • Keep asking: "What power of the base gives the argument?"
  • Connect each logarithm law to an exponent law students already know.
  • Check domains when solving logarithmic equations.
  • Use exact log form before decimal calculator approximations.

Common Student Mistakes

  • Writing log(x + y) as log(x) + log(y)
  • Mixing up the base and the argument
  • Forgetting to bring powers down as coefficients
  • Taking logarithms of zero or negative values

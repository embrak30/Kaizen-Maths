Bearings Guidance

Overview

This tool develops three-figure bearings using clear diagrams. Students practise reading bearings clockwise from north, reversing bearings, interpreting hidden angle descriptions, and using simple trigonometry in navigation contexts.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Read bearings from diagrams and find reverse bearings.
  • Level 2: Convert hidden direction descriptions into bearings and combine this with reverse bearings.
  • Level 3: Use right-triangle trigonometry to connect bearings with distances and horizontal or vertical components.

Key Ideas

  • A bearing is measured clockwise from north.
  • Bearings are written with three figures, so 35° becomes 035°.
  • Reverse bearings differ by 180°.
  • In trigonometry problems, the east-west component uses sine of the bearing angle, and the north-south component uses cosine of the bearing angle.

Common Misconceptions

  • Measuring the angle from the wrong north line.
  • Writing 35° instead of the three-figure bearing 035°.
  • Adding 180° when the answer goes beyond 360° without subtracting 360°.
  • Mixing up the sine and cosine components in bearing problems.

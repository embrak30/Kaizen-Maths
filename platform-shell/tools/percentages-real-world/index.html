Real-World Percentages

Getting Started

This tool demonstrates percentage applications across everyday, financial, business, scientific, and statistical contexts.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Shopping discounts, tips, service charges, and basic taxes.
  • Level 2: Sales commission, simple interest, business markup, and profit margin.
  • Level 3: Compound interest, loans, investments, mortgages, and returns.
  • Level 4: Concentration, statistical intervals, and measurement error.

Key Formulas

  • Simple interest: I = PRT.
  • Compound interest: A = P(1 + r)^t.
  • Commission: commission = sales × rate.
  • Percentage change: difference ÷ original × 100.
  • Concentration: amount ÷ total × 100.

Teaching Strategies

  • Ask students to identify the base quantity before calculating.
  • Connect each context to decisions students may recognise, such as discounts and borrowing.
  • Discuss why rounding to two decimal places matters in money contexts.
  • Compare additive methods with multiplier methods where both are useful.

Common Mistakes

  • Applying tax before discount when the problem asks for discount first.
  • Using the original amount instead of the new amount in the second stage of a multi-step calculation.
  • Confusing markup with profit margin.
  • Forgetting to convert annual interest rates into monthly rates for loan calculations.

Using the Timer

  • Use 2 minutes for daily applications and simple interest.
  • Use 5 minutes for finance and scientific contexts with several steps.
  • Reveal steps after students have explained which quantities they used and why.

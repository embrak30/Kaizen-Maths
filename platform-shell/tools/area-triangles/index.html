Area of Triangles Guidance

Overview

This tool gives repeated diagram practice for perimeter and area of triangles. Area examples focus on identifying the base and perpendicular height.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Perimeter and right-triangle area using a given base and height.
  • Level 2: Area of triangles with an internal dashed altitude, including scalene and isosceles examples.
  • Level 3: External altitudes, reverse area problems, and mixed area practice.

Key Ideas

  • Perimeter: Add the side lengths around the outside.
  • Area: Area = 1/2 × base × height.
  • Height: The height must be perpendicular to the base, even when it is drawn outside the triangle.

Common Misconceptions

  • Using a slanted side as the height.
  • Forgetting to halve base × height.
  • Adding dimensions when the question asks for area.
  • Ignoring an external altitude on obtuse triangles.

Circle Theorems Guidance

Overview

This tool practises standard circle theorems through clean diagrams, missing-angle calculations, algebraic angle problems, and short proof-style reasoning.

Key Theorems

  • The angle at the centre is twice the angle at the circumference standing on the same arc.
  • The angle in a semicircle is 90 degrees.
  • Angles in the same segment are equal.
  • Opposite angles in a cyclic quadrilateral add to 180 degrees.
  • The tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact.
  • The angle between a tangent and a chord equals the angle in the alternate segment.

Teaching Focus

  • Ask students to name the theorem before calculating.
  • Insist that students identify the same arc or same chord when using angle theorems.
  • For multi-step problems, get students to mark every new angle found before moving on.
  • For proof questions, the reason matters as much as the final angle statement.

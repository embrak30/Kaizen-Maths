Teacher Instructions

Purpose

This tool generates hypothesis testing practice with detailed inference. It focuses on stating hypotheses clearly, choosing the correct tail, calculating a p-value or critical region, and making a contextual conclusion.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Exact binomial hypothesis tests, including one-tailed, two-tailed, and critical-region decisions.
  • Level 2: Normal tests for a population mean where the standard deviation is known or a large-sample normal approximation is being used.
  • Level 3: Proportion z-tests, model-choice questions, and mixed contextual tests.

Inference Structure

  • Define the parameter being tested.
  • State H0 and H1.
  • Use the model assuming H0 is true.
  • Calculate the p-value or critical region.
  • Compare with the significance level.
  • Write the final conclusion in the language of the question.

Teacher Instructions

Purpose

This tool generates practice on Newton's second law, resultant force, weight, friction, tension, connected particles, and inclined-plane problems.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Direct use of F = ma, including resultant force, acceleration, mass, and weight.
  • Level 2: Forces in one dimension, including driving force, resistance, friction, and tension.
  • Level 3: Connected particles, lifts, and inclined planes where the force equation needs careful modelling.

Teaching Notes

  • Always identify the direction of acceleration before writing the force equation.
  • Use resultant force = mass x acceleration, not just total force = ma.
  • For weight, use W = mg, with g = 9.8 m s-2 unless another value is stated.
  • For friction, use F = μR only when a coefficient of friction is given.
  • For connected particles, write one force equation for the whole system or one equation for each particle.

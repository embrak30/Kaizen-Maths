Teacher Instructions

Purpose

This tool supports practice with binomial and geometric distributions, including exact probabilities, cumulative probabilities, complements, expected values, and simple modelling decisions.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Binomial distribution basics, exact probabilities, cumulative probabilities, and mixed binomial sets.
  • Level 2: Geometric distribution basics, first success probabilities, cumulative probabilities, and mixed geometric sets.
  • Level 3: Mixed binomial and geometric modelling, complements, expected values, and finding parameters from probability statements.

Teaching Notes

  • Students should decide whether the number of trials is fixed before choosing the binomial model.
  • For geometric questions, reinforce that X is the trial number on which the first success occurs.
  • Worked steps show the formula substitution before giving the numerical answer.
  • Mixed sets are useful for checking whether students can choose the correct distribution rather than just apply a formula.

Common Mistakes

  • Using geometric probability when the question has a fixed number of trials.
  • Forgetting the combination term in binomial exact probabilities.
  • Confusing P(X < r), P(X ≤ r), and P(X ≥ r).
  • Using p instead of 1 - p for failures before the first success.

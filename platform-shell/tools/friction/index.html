Teacher Instructions

Purpose

This tool generates mechanics practice on friction, normal reaction, coefficient of friction, limiting equilibrium, rough horizontal surfaces, and rough inclined planes.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Direct friction formulae, normal reaction, limiting friction, and coefficient of friction.
  • Level 2: Rough horizontal motion, acceleration, stopping distance, and pulling at an angle.
  • Level 3: Rough inclined planes, limiting equilibrium, sliding, and forces parallel to a slope.

Teaching Notes

  • Friction acts parallel to the contact surface and opposes motion or the tendency to move.
  • For limiting friction, use F = μR.
  • For non-limiting static friction, use F ≤ μR.
  • On a horizontal surface with no vertical applied force, R = mg.
  • On a slope, resolve weight into mg sin θ down the slope and mg cos θ perpendicular to the slope.

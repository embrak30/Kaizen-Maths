Area Models

Purpose

Use area models to connect multiplication, fractions, percentages, expanding brackets, and factorising to a visible rectangular structure.

Teaching Flow

  • Start with the rectangle before revealing calculations.
  • Ask what the sides or partitions represent.
  • Reveal one step at a time so pupils connect each cell to a product or part of a whole.
  • Move from the visual model to the formal written calculation.

Good Questions

  • What does each side length tell us?
  • Why must the smaller rectangles add to the total area?
  • Which parts of the model are equal, and which parts are different?
  • How does this area model explain the algebraic method?

Common Misconceptions

  • Adding side lengths instead of multiplying to find area.
  • Partitioning one side correctly but forgetting to multiply every pair of partitions.
  • Forgetting to collect like terms after expanding brackets.
  • Using an area model decoratively without checking that each cell has a meaning.

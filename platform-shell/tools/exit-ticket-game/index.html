Teacher Guide

This choice board gives students a quick, low-stakes reflective prompt at the end of a mathematics lesson.

How To Use It

  • Project the colour board at the end of the lesson.
  • Ask one student, a group, or the class to choose a colour.
  • Click the colour to reveal a prompt and start the five-minute timer.
  • Students respond in books, on mini-whiteboards, verbally, or digitally.

Useful routine: Keep the prompt unknown until a colour is chosen. It makes the task feel fresh and reduces students trying to pick an easier reflection.

Prompt Types

  • Understanding and confidence checks.
  • Misconception spotting and help requests.
  • Connections to earlier learning or real-world use.
  • Metacognition: strategies, effort, mistakes, and next steps.

Using The Responses

  • Choose one response to open the next lesson.
  • Group students by support need.
  • Spot where a topic needs another example or a different representation.
  • Save strong responses as models for future classes.

Fractions Teacher Instructions

Getting Started

This tool builds fraction confidence from equivalent fractions and simplifying into operations with like denominators, unlike denominators, and mixed numbers.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Equivalent fractions using multiplication, division, and cross multiplication.
  • Level 2: Simplifying fractions by finding common factors.
  • Level 3: Adding and subtracting when denominators already match.
  • Level 4: All four operations with proper fractions and mixed numbers.

Teaching Focus

  • Use diagrams or fraction bars before moving to symbolic methods.
  • Ask students to explain why denominators stay the same for like-denominator addition and subtraction.
  • Build the habit of simplifying final answers.
  • For mixed numbers, connect the conversion to improper fractions with the size of the whole.

Common Misconceptions

  • Adding denominators instead of finding a common denominator.
  • Forgetting to simplify after calculating.
  • Using the reciprocal of the first fraction in division rather than the second.
  • Losing the whole-number part when converting mixed numbers.

Using the Timer

  • Use 2 minutes for equivalent fractions and simplifying.
  • Use 5 minutes for mixed operations or full worked examples.
  • Reveal steps after students have explained their chosen method.

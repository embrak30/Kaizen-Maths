Poisson Distribution

Overview

This tool builds Poisson distribution fluency through exact probabilities, cumulative probabilities, complements, rates, independent sums, conditional probabilities, and approximation modelling.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Formula use, exact probabilities, cumulative probabilities, complements, mean and variance.
  • Level 2: Context questions involving rates, adjusted time intervals, at most, at least, and between statements.
  • Level 3: Independent Poisson variables, sums, conditional probabilities, and parameter questions.
  • Level 4: Poisson approximation to binomial and mixed model-choice questions.

Teaching Focus

  • For X ~ Po(lambda), both the mean and variance are lambda.
  • Students should state the model and parameter before substituting into the formula.
  • Use complements for at least questions to avoid long sums.
  • Independent Poisson variables add: if X and Y are independent, X + Y is Po(lambda1 + lambda2).

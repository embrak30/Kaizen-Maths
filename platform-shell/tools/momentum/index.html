Teacher Instructions

Purpose

This tool generates mechanics practice on momentum, impulse, conservation of momentum, direct collisions, explosions, recoil, and coefficient of restitution.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Momentum and impulse basics using p = mv, impulse = Ft, and impulse = change in momentum.
  • Level 2: Conservation of momentum in one-dimensional collisions, particles joining, explosions, and recoil.
  • Level 3: Impulse in collisions and direct collisions involving coefficient of restitution.

Teaching Notes

  • Always choose a positive direction before using signs for velocity.
  • Momentum is a vector quantity, so direction matters.
  • Impulse equals change in momentum and has units N s or kg m s-1.
  • In a closed system, total momentum before equals total momentum after.
  • For direct impact, e = speed of separation ÷ speed of approach.
p = mv Impulse = Ft Impulse = mv - mu momentum before = momentum after e = separation ÷ approach

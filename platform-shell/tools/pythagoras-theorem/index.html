Pythagoras Theorem Guidance

Overview

This tool builds Pythagoras theorem from identifying the hypotenuse through missing side calculations, exact surd answers, and area or perimeter applications.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Find the hypotenuse, find a shorter side, and mix basic integer triples.
  • Level 2: Work with exact surd form, decimal approximations, and varied units.
  • Level 3: Find perimeters and areas where Pythagoras is needed first.

Key Ideas

  • Formula: a^2 + b^2 = c^2, where c is the hypotenuse.
  • Hypotenuse: Add the squares of the two shorter sides.
  • Shorter side: Subtract the square of the known shorter side from the hypotenuse square.
  • Exact form: Leave non-square roots as simplified surds when requested.

Common Misconceptions

  • Using the longest drawn side instead of the side opposite the right angle.
  • Adding when finding a shorter side instead of subtracting.
  • Rounding too early before perimeter or area calculations.
  • Writing square-root answers without units.

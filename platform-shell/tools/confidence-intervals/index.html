Confidence Intervals

Overview

This tool generates confidence interval practice for means, proportions, margin of error, sample size, interpretation, and t-intervals.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Confidence intervals for a mean when the population standard deviation is known.
  • Level 2: Confidence intervals for proportions from survey or sample data.
  • Level 3: Margin of error, sample size, confidence level, and interpretation.
  • Level 4: t-intervals for a mean when the population standard deviation is unknown.

Teaching Focus

  • Students should identify the statistic, standard error, critical value, and margin of error.
  • The interval is estimate plus or minus margin of error.
  • Interpretations should refer to the population parameter, not the sample statistic.
  • For unknown population standard deviation, use the sample standard deviation and a t critical value.

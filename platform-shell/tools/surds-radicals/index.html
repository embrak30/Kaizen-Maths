√ Teacher Instructions

Getting Started

This tool demonstrates operations with surds and irrational roots. Students simplify, multiply, divide, combine like surds, rationalise denominators, and expand brackets.

Level Guide

  • Level 1 - Fundamentals: Simplifying surds, perfect square factors, and simplest surd form
  • Level 2 - Operations: Multiplying, dividing, adding, subtracting, and combining multiple surd terms
  • Level 3 - Advanced: Rationalising denominators, expanding brackets, conjugates, difference of two squares, and multi-step expressions

Key Surd Rules

  • Simplification: √(ab) = √a × √b
  • Multiplication: √a × √b = √(ab)
  • Division: √a ÷ √b = √(a/b)
  • Like surds: 2√3 + 5√3 = 7√3
  • Rationalising: Use √b/√b or the conjugate to remove a surd from the denominator

Teaching Strategies

  • Emphasise identifying the largest perfect square factor.
  • Keep coefficients and surd parts separate while simplifying.
  • Check whether surds are like terms before adding or subtracting.
  • Use conjugates for denominators such as a + √b.

Common Mistakes

  • Trying to combine unlike surds such as √2 + √3
  • Multiplying coefficients but forgetting to multiply the radicands
  • Leaving a denominator containing a surd after rationalising
  • Sign errors when expanding binomial brackets

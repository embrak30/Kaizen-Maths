Teacher Instructions

Purpose

This tool generates practice on constant acceleration equations of motion, including direct SUVAT substitution, braking distance, multi-step motion, and vertical motion under gravity.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Choose and use one equation of motion when four quantities are known.
  • Level 2: Combine equations, interpret braking problems, and handle two-stage journeys.
  • Level 3: Use signs carefully for vertical motion under gravity, maximum height, time of flight, and speed at a height.

Teaching Notes

  • Encourage students to list the known SUVAT values before choosing an equation.
  • Braking distance is the distance travelled while the vehicle is slowing down after the brakes are applied.
  • Stopping distance is the total distance needed to stop: thinking distance + braking distance.
  • For vertical motion, state the positive direction before substituting values.
  • Distances are in metres, speeds in m/s, acceleration in m/s², and time in seconds unless stated otherwise.

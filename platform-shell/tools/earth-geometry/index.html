Earth Geometry Guidance

Overview

This tool practises geometry on the surface of the Earth using latitude, longitude, meridians, parallels, great circles, small circles, and time-zone links.

Key Ideas

  • Distances along a meridian use the full Earth radius because meridians are great circles.
  • Distances along the equator also use the full Earth radius.
  • Distances along a parallel of latitude use the smaller radius R cos(latitude).
  • Longitude differences can be linked to time because Earth turns 360 degrees in 24 hours.

Teaching Focus

  • Ask students to decide first whether the route is along a meridian, the equator, or a parallel.
  • Make students write the angular difference before substituting into an arc-length formula.
  • For parallel questions, check that students use R cos(latitude), not R.
  • For time questions, connect 15 degrees of longitude with 1 hour.

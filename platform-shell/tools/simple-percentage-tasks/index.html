Percentage Teacher Instructions

Getting Started

This tool helps students practise percentage skills in a structured progression from conversions through applied percentage change.

Level Guide

  • Level 1: Convert between percentages, decimals, and fractions.
  • Level 2: Convert fractions and quantities into percentages, and find simple percentages of amounts.
  • Level 3: Calculate percentages of quantities, increases, and decreases.
  • Level 4: Solve multi-step, reverse percentage, and combined discount or VAT problems.

Key Concepts

  • Percentage means parts per hundred.
  • Percentage to decimal: divide by 100.
  • Decimal to percentage: multiply by 100.
  • Percentage of a quantity: convert to a decimal and multiply.
  • Percentage change: compare the difference with the original amount.

Teaching Strategies

  • Keep returning to the meaning of "out of 100".
  • Use familiar benchmarks such as 10%, 25%, 50%, and 75%.
  • Ask students to identify the original amount before percentage change questions.
  • Use multipliers to connect increase, decrease, and reverse percentage methods.

Common Mistakes

  • Moving the decimal point in the wrong direction.
  • Forgetting to multiply by 100 when converting to a percentage.
  • Using the final amount instead of the original amount in percentage change.
  • Treating a 20% decrease followed by a 20% increase as a return to the original value.

Using the Timer

  • Use 2 minutes for conversions and quick fluency.
  • Use 5 minutes for multi-step and reverse percentage tasks.
  • Reveal steps once students have explained their chosen method.
